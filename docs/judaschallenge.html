Visualizing the Elite





Motivation



Every time Chris Jericho makes his entrance into the ring and the song "Judas" plays, the entire arena would sing along. Earlier this week, fans on Reddit and Twitter mentioned that they wanted to virtually recreate this. They encouraged others to post videos of them singing Judas during Dynamite and use the hashtags #JudasChallenge and #WorldWideCrowd to share on Twitter.

The AEW Dynamite Twitter Activity analysis showed that eventhough AEW Dynamite is being aired in a closed location with no audience, the Judas Challenge/Experiment was a creative attempt to recreate this crowd phenomenom in a different form. Judas Challenge/Experiment. Here's a summary of the findings.

Twitter Fans Sing Judas as Chris Jericho Enters the Ring



The tweets were collected on Mar 25, 2020 from 05:59PM CST to 10:19PM CST, for approximately 4 hours and 19 min. (See data collection for more details.) This line graph shows the number of tweets that mention either #JudasChallenge or #WorldWideCrowd over the span of time. The vertical black dotted lines are reference lines for when the show started 07:00PM CST (or 19:00) and when it concluded 09:00PM CST (or 21:00). The vertical green dotted line (08:45PM CST or 20:48) is when @AEWonTNT sent a tweet encouraging the fans on Twitter to sing along as Chris Jericho is about to make his entrance.



As shown in the graph, Twitter fans were tweeting and retweeting renditions of Judas throughout the evening. The blue line corresponds to the total number of tweets that mentioned either hashtags. The red line corresponds to tweets that were not retweets. In other words, they contained original content in their tweets. There's a large spike in Twitter activity at 8:48pm CST with 34 tweets, just minutes after AEW's tweet. However, most of that activity consists of retweets and not necessarily original content.

Data Collection




Here's a summary of the technologies used in creating this project.

  • Data collection and storage: Twitter API, Tweepy, Python, PostgreSQL
  • Data processing and analysis: Python, NumPy, Pandas, RE, nltk
  • Visualization and dashboard: Matplotlib, Plot.ly, Dash, Heroku, Bootstrap

Query terms that were tracked on Twitter using the Streaming API: #AEW, #AllEliteWrestling, #AEWDark, #AEWDynamite, #AEWonTNT, #WWE, #NXT
The dataset used for the Judas Challenge analysis is a subset of the larger dataset that contains all tweets collected using the list of query terms above.
